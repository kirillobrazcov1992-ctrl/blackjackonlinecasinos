Blackjack Rules — How to Play

Everything you need to know to start playing blackjack with confidence.

Blackjack Rules Overview

Online casinos offer many different software providers and blackjack game variations. They use a minimum of one deck and maximum of eight. Eight-deck games give a far greater advantage for the house — while single-deck games are extremely favorable to card counters, even amateur ones. In single-deck games, the dealer hits on soft 17 as compensation. We recommend choosing games with the minimum number of decks or standard 4-6 deck games where the dealer stands on 17.

How a Hand is Played

Players place their bets in the boxes directly in front of their seat. Each player receives two cards face up, while the dealer gets one card face up and one card either face down or placed alongside. Players then say "hit" to receive additional cards or "stand", "stay", or "no more" to stop. If a player goes over 21 (busts), they lose immediately.

After all players have finished, the dealer plays their hand. If your hand is closer to 21 than the dealer's, you win. If it's lower, you lose. If it's a tie, it's a push and your bet is returned.

The Ace can be counted as either 1 or 11 depending on what benefits the player. An Ace helps you reach 21 faster and gives you flexibility — it's the most valuable card in the game.

Natural Blackjack: If your first two cards are a 10-value card and an Ace, you've got a "natural" or "blackjack". Do not split this combination. Blackjacks pay out 3:2 in the standard game (some variations may differ). If you reach 21 with more than two cards, it's not considered a blackjack — it pays even money.

Soft Hand: A hand containing an Ace on the initial deal is called a "soft" hand because you can draw additional cards without the risk of going bust. Always play basic blackjack strategy according to the strategy chart.

Player Options

Stand (Stay)

You are satisfied with the value of your hand and do not wish to receive any more cards. Your hand count stays the same and play passes to the next player or dealer.

Hit

You want additional cards to get closer to a count of 21. You may take as many cards as you choose until you either stand or go bust (exceed 21).

Double Down

A strategic move where you double your original bet and receive exactly one more card. In many variations this is permitted only on 9, 10, or 11 — while others allow it only on 10 and 11, or just 11. Some variations allow doubling after three cards or after a split, and some even allow doubling on any two starting cards.

Split

If your first two cards are of equal value, you can split them into two separate hands and match your original bet. Each hand is then played independently. Some variations allow re-splitting when you receive another equal card, up to four hands. When splitting Aces, you typically receive only one additional card per hand.

Surrender

The option to forfeit your hand if you think your cards have poor chances. Not available at all tables. When surrendered, you lose half your bet. Usually only available after the first two cards are dealt.

The Dealer's Hand

After all players have played, the dealer plays their hand according to the casino's rules. Typically, the dealer must hit all hands equivalent to sixteen (16) or fewer, and stand on every hand equal to seventeen (17). This applies to both soft and hard 17, though the rule can vary — at some sites the dealer hits on soft 17. The rules are usually indicated on the table layout above the betting area.

Soft vs Hard Hands: A hand containing an Ace that can be drawn to without busting is called "soft". A "hard" hand may contain an Ace but it counts as 1 because 11 would cause a bust. After the dealer finishes, winning hands are paid or chips are collected. In a push, chips stay on the table.

Blackjack Winning Hand Payouts

Even Money (1:1)

Most hands are paid at even money — 1:1. This applies to each bet, each split, and each doubled hand.

Natural Blackjack (3:2)

If your first two cards total 21, that's a blackjack or natural twenty-one. It pays 3:2 (one and a half times your bet). For example: a $3 bet pays $4.50 ($3 + $1.50); $15 pays $22.50; $20 pays $30; $75 pays $112.50. Some casinos may have different payout structures, so check conditions beforehand.

Insurance (2:1)

Insurance is a side-bet available when the dealer's face-up card is an Ace. It's completely optional. If the dealer has blackjack, insurance pays 2:1. For example: a $10 insurance bet pays $20; $25 pays $50. If the dealer doesn't have blackjack, only the insurance bet is lost — not your main wager. To cover your entire bet, you'd place half of it on insurance.
