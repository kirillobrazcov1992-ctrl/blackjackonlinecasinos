Key Strategy Principles

  • Always assume the dealer's hole card is 10. This is the single most important rule in blackjack strategy.
  • Never take insurance. It's a sucker bet — the house edge on insurance is over 7%.
  • Stand on 12-16 if the dealer shows 2-6. Let the dealer bust.
  • Always split Aces and 8s. Never split 10s or 5s.
  • Double down on 11 regardless of the dealer's up card.

Advanced Techniques

Card Counting

Card counting tracks the ratio of high cards to low cards remaining in the deck. When the count is high (more 10s and Aces left), the player has an advantage and should increase bets. The Hi-Lo system is the most popular method: +1 for 2-6, 0 for 7-9, -1 for 10-Ace.

Card counting is legal but casinos can ban you if caught. It's most effective in single-deck or double-deck games.

Bankroll Management

Never bet more than 1-2% of your total bankroll per hand. Set win and loss limits before you sit down. If you're playing with $1,000, your bet should be between $10 and $20 per hand. This ensures you can weather losing streaks.

Doubling Strategy

If you have a 10 and the dealer has 9 or below — plus 11 — it is safe to double down. This is one of the most profitable moves in blackjack when applied correctly. Combine this with the basic strategy chart for maximum effectiveness.
