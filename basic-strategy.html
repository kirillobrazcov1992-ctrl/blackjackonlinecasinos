Blackjack Basic Strategy

The mathematically optimal way to play every hand. Master these charts and reduce the house edge to under 0.5%.

Basic strategy is a mathematically derived set of rules that tells you the optimal decision — hit, stand, double down, split, or surrender — for every possible hand combination against every dealer upcard. It was first calculated in 1956 by four Army mathematicians and later refined by MIT professor Edward O. Thorp. Using basic strategy, the house edge drops from 2–3% (for typical player intuition) to just 0.17–0.66%, depending on the specific rules.

How to read these charts: Find your hand in the left column, find the dealer's upcard across the top row, and the cell where they meet tells you the optimal play. H = Hit, S = Stand, D = Double Down, P = Split, RH = Surrender if allowed (otherwise Hit), RS = Surrender if allowed (otherwise Stand).

How to Memorize Basic Strategy

Learning basic strategy by heart may seem daunting, but most players pick it up in a few hours of practice. Here are some simple rules of thumb to get you started:

Hard Hands — The Golden Rule

Stand on 17 or higher no matter what. Stand on 13–16 against dealer 2–6. Hit on 12 against dealer 2, 3, or 7+. Double on 11 always. Double on 10 against 2–9. Double on 9 against 3–6.

Soft Hands — The Ace Advantage

Never hit a soft 19 or 20. Always double soft 17 against dealer 3–6. Double soft 15–16 against dealer 4–6. Double soft 13–14 against dealer 5–6. Against everything else — just hit until you have a strong hard hand.

Pairs — The Split Decision

Always split Aces and 8s. Never split 10s or 5s. Split 9s against everything except 7, 10, Ace. Split 2s, 3s, and 7s against dealer 2–7. Split 6s against dealer 2–6.

Common Mistakes to Avoid: Taking insurance (it's a losing bet in the long run). Standing on 12–16 against a dealer 7+. Not splitting 8s against any dealer upcard. Playing by "gut feeling" instead of the math. Assuming the dealer is "due" for a bust.

Advanced Strategy Considerations

Basic strategy charts are calculated for specific rule sets. If you're playing a game with different rules, the optimal play may change slightly. Key rule variations that affect strategy:

• Dealer hits soft 17 (H17): The house edge increases by about 0.2%. Strategy adjustments are minor but do exist — notably, you should double soft 18 against dealer 2, and surrender more aggressively.

• Double after split (DAS): When allowed (most casinos allow it), this slightly favors the player. Some borderline splits become profitable only when DAS is permitted.

• Surrender available: Late surrender reduces the house edge by about 0.08%. Use it on hard 16 against dealer 9, 10, Ace and hard 15 against dealer 10.

Pro Tip: Print out this basic strategy chart and keep it next to you when you play online blackjack. Having the chart visible on a second screen is perfectly legal and will immediately improve your game. Most online casinos don't mind — they'd rather you play basic strategy than count cards!

Practice Makes Perfect

The best way to learn basic strategy is to practice. Play free blackjack games online with the strategy chart next to you, and before long, the decisions will become second nature. Most experienced players don't even think about basic strategy — it becomes instinct through repetition.

Once you've mastered basic strategy, you'll be ready to explore more advanced techniques like card counting and game variation strategy.
