Blackjack Variations

There's more than one way to play 21. Discover the different blackjack variants, their rules, and which ones give you the best odds.

While all blackjack games share the same core objective — beat the dealer without busting — the specific rules can vary dramatically. Some variants favor the player, while others are designed to increase the house edge. Here's everything you need to know.

Classic Blackjack Most Popular

6–8 decks · Dealer stands on 17 · 3:2 blackjack payout

The standard blackjack game found in most casinos and online platforms. Played with 6 or 8 decks, the dealer stands on all 17s (including soft 17 in many versions). Players can double down on any first two cards, double after splitting is typically allowed, and insurance is offered when the dealer shows an Ace.

✓ Lowest house edge (~0.5% with basic strategy)✓ Widely available✓ Best for beginners

European Blackjack Classic

2 decks · Dealer stands on 17 · No hole card

The key difference in European Blackjack: the dealer receives only one card face up initially (no hole card). The dealer only takes their second card after all players have acted. This means you can't double down or split if the dealer would have blackjack — and if the dealer gets blackjack after you've doubled or split, you lose both your original and additional bets.

European Blackjack is typically played with 2 decks. The no-hole-card rule adds a slight edge to the house, but the game remains popular across European casinos.

✓ Only 2 decks (good for counters)✗ No hole card rule✗ Slightly higher house edge

Spanish 21 Player-Friendly

6–8 decks (no 10s) · Liberal rules

Spanish 21 is played with a Spanish deck — all 10s are removed. This would seem to favor the house, but the game compensates with extremely liberal rules: late surrender, doubling after split on any number of cards, re-splitting Aces, and special bonus payouts for 5+ card 21s, 6-7-8 combinations, and 7-7-7. The combination of rules often gives Spanish 21 a lower house edge than standard blackjack, despite having no 10s.

✓ Very player-friendly rules✓ Bonus payouts✓ Can have lower house edge✗ No 10s in the deck

Blackjack Switch

6–8 decks · Players can swap cards between two hands

In Blackjack Switch, you play two hands simultaneously and can switch the second card between them. For example, if Hand 1 has 6+5 = 11 and Hand 2 has 9+3 = 12, you can switch to create 6+3 = 9 and 9+5 = 14 — or any other combination you prefer. The trade-off: a dealer 22 pushes (ties) all non-busted player hands instead of busting.

✓ Creative strategic element✓ Popular in online casinos✗ Dealer 22 is a push

Double Exposure Blackjack

Both dealer cards face up · Blackjack pays even money

In Double Exposure, both of the dealer's cards are visible to the player — a massive informational advantage. However, to compensate, blackjack pays even money (1:1) instead of 3:2, the dealer wins all ties (except player blackjack), and insurance is not offered. The transparency is useful for learning, but the rule changes offset most of the advantage.

✓ Both dealer cards visible✗ Blackjack pays 1:1✗ Dealer wins ties

Pontoon British Classic

British variant · Unique terminology

Pontoon is the British version of blackjack with its own terminology: a "pontoon" (21 with two cards) pays more, a "five-card trick" (5 cards without busting) is a winning hand, and the dealer's cards are both dealt face down. Players can "twist" (hit), "stick" (stand), "buy" (double — but costs exactly the same as your bet), and split once. Pontoon offers some of the best odds of any casino game when played optimally.

✓ Excellent odds✓ Five-card trick bonus✗ Unfamiliar terminology for Americans

Perfect Pairs Blackjack

Side bet on pair outcomes

Perfect Pairs is standard blackjack with an optional side bet that pays out if your first two cards form a pair. Payouts vary: a "perfect pair" (same rank and suit) pays the most, followed by "colored pair" (same rank and color), and "mixed pair" (same rank, different suits). The side bet carries a high house edge (typically 10%+) and should be treated as entertainment, not a serious strategy.

✓ Fun side action✗ High house edge on side bet

Infinite Blackjack Modern

Unlimited players · Evolution Gaming

Pioneered by Evolution Gaming for live dealer platforms, Infinite Blackjack allows an unlimited number of players to join a single table simultaneously. It uses a "everyone plays the same hand" mechanic but adds optional "Any Pair" and "Hot 3" side bets. The game also features "Six Card Charlie" — automatically win if you draw 6 cards without busting. It's fast, social, and perfect for live dealer fans.

✓ No waiting for seats✓ Six Card Charlie rule✓ Social live dealer experience

Choosing the Right Variant

Not all blackjack games are created equal. When choosing a variant, look for these player-friendly rules:

  • 3:2 blackjack payout — Avoid 6:5 games at all costs (they increase the house edge by ~1.4%)
  • Dealer stands on soft 17 — Reduces the house edge by ~0.2%
  • Double after split (DAS) allowed — A significant player advantage
  • Late surrender available — Small but useful option
  • Single or double deck — Fewer decks = lower house edge

If a game has 6:5 blackjack, avoid it regardless of any other rules — it's simply not worth playing. Always read the rules before sitting down at a table (or clicking "Play").
